You enter a room with an ominous red light, on the back wall of the room, there's a recipe engraved in the bricks.

The recipe reads:

            "Ingredients needed:
            Warm water: (105-115 degrees)- to activate the yeast.
            Active Dry yeast: Instant or rapid rise yeast can be substituted, following my adaption notes in
            the recipe card.
            Granulated sugar or honey: the sugar is used to “feed” the yeast and tenderize the bread.
            Salt: to enhance flavor
            Oil: Vegetable or canola oil, or melted butter could be substituted
            Flour: Bread Flour or All-Purpose Flour can both be used with no changes to the recipe. The exact 
            amount of flour used will vary depending on different factors (altitude/humidity etc.). What
            matters is the texture of the dough. It should be smooth and pull away from the sides of the bowl.
            It’s important not to add too much flour or your bread will be dense. The dough should be just
            slightly sticky when touched with a clean finger."
